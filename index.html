Building BLOT.WORLD: A Modern Portfolio Experience

A deep dive into the design decisions, technical challenges, and creative process behind building my personal portfolio website from scratch.

Web Design HTML CSS JavaScript UX/UI

The Challenge

Every creative professional faces the same dilemma: how do you showcase your diverse skills and projects in a way that's both engaging and authentic? After exploring countless portfolio templates and platforms, I realized none captured what I wanted to communicate. I needed something unique, something that felt like me.

The goal was clear: create a digital space that serves as both a professional portfolio and a personal archive—a place where web design, branding projects, digital art, photography, and even poetry could coexist harmoniously.

Design Philosophy

Dark Mode by Default

I opted for a dark theme as the foundation. Not just because it's trendy, but because it allows colors, images, and interactive elements to truly pop. The deep black background creates a gallery-like atmosphere where each project becomes a illuminated exhibit.

Typography as Expression

I chose Roboto Flex for its variable font capabilities. This single typeface can shift from condensed and sharp to wide and expressive, matching the tone of each section.

Technical Implementation

Vanilla JavaScript Philosophy

No frameworks. No build tools. Just clean, purposeful vanilla JavaScript. This decision was intentional for performance, control, learning value, and longevity.

Unique Features

Inception Protection Modal

One of my favorite touches is the "Inception Protection" feature. When users try to click on the portfolio project card while viewing the portfolio, they get a playful Windows XP-style error message with a nostalgic error sound.

Lessons Learned

Building BLOT.WORLD reinforced several important principles: start with content, progressive enhancement, test early and often, and iterate ruthlessly.

Your portfolio is more than a resume—it's a statement about who you are as a creator. The best portfolio is one that reflects not just what you can do, but how you think.
